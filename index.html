Charles Dickens

Charles Dickens was born on 7th of February 1812 in Portsmouth, England. His father was a clerk, unfortunately, he was sent to jail for debts when Charles was just a little child.

Charles went to work at a blacking factory when he was 12, to earn money for his family. The harsh living conditions and the severe poverty Charles experienced, shaped his thoughts and beliefs.

He received an average education, yet he struggled to teach himself immensely.
He managed to work as a parliamentary reporter in one of the newspapers.

In 1843, one of Dickens’ most famous works was published – 'A Christmas Carol'. In this story, it was particularly clear that Dickens was becoming increasingly disappointed with the social philosophy of his time
