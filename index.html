Charles Dickens

Charles Dickens was born on 7th of February 1812 in Portsmouth, England. His father was a clerk, unfortunately, he was sent to jail for debts when Charles was just a little child.

Charles went to work at a blacking factory when he was 12, to earn money for his family. The harsh living conditions and the severe poverty Charles experienced, shaped his thoughts and beliefs.

He received an average education, yet he struggled to teach himself immensely.
He managed to work as a parliamentary reporter in one of the newspapers.

In 1843, one of Dickens’ most famous works was published – 'A Christmas Carol'. In this story, it was particularly clear that Dickens was becoming increasingly disappointed with the social philosophy of his time

Much that was bad in society, he believed, was a result of an obsession with money and the social status that came from money.
Dickens travelled abroad in the 1840s, first to America and then to Europe. A new stage in his life began. His novels were more serious and more carefully planned. ‘David Copperfield’, published 1849-50, was similar to the story of Dickens’ own life.
Readers of the novel experienced the same imperfect world that Dickens had experienced.

Introduction

Charles Dickens wrote a number of great novels, but David Copperfield was his favourite and portrayed his own life, and his miserable childhood after his father was imprisoned.
The story revolves around a young boy under the care of his mother until he has his own family. David meets a lot of people as he grows up, studies, and works. Some people are very cruel to him.

He has been looked after by Peggotty, the servant of his family, Mr. Murdstone, his stepfather, and Betsy Trotwood, his aunt. He struggled a lot after the death of his mother.
